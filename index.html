Brown Planaria(Dugesia tigrina)

Background

Planaria are flatworms from the class Turbellaria and phylum Platyhelminthes. They have highly effective regenerative abilities due to the neoblasts contained within their tissue. When they are cut, neoblasts gather near the injured area. Neoblasts are the pluripotent stem cells found in planaria. Unlike many other organisms, planaria retain pluripotent cells for their entire lifespan. Ordinarily, pluripotent stem cells are only present during the embryonic stage and are not present in large amounts in adults. Yet the neoblasts in planaria remain and also have high basal mitotic activity. This study will make use of the planaria's regenerative abilities to observe memory through regeneration.

Rationale

At the moment, there are not many recent studies on planaria memory. Many years ago in the 1960s, studies demonstrating the phenomenon of planaria memory retention after bisection were conducted. However, now, there are few studies on that discovery. So, this study will further the understanding of planaria memory after regeneration. Data about memory storage in planaria will also be helpful with future planaria research. With planaria, it will be possible to observe memory during brain regeneration. Normally, memory is stored in the brain, so if the brain is damaged, memory is lost. But in planaria, memory is somehow retained, even when the entire brain is removed. So, memory preservation can be observed outside the brain, in the planaria's tissue.
